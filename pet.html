What’s the biggest problem?

Pet finder can’t find the one

Facts: Surveys showing around there are 17M pet finders each year, they add about 6M pets each year, yet 2.7M healthy animals are euthanized in shelters because they are not adopted in time

Why?

There are many possible reasons:

Before visiting shelters

–They are not familiar with shelters, and it seems a lot effort to go through the adoption process

–They have some concerns about animals in shelter: breed, health, age, history, etc.

–They don’t know enough infomation about selecting a pet

–They don’t know the pet in shelters would suit them

–They think the shelters don’t have the pet they are looking for

When visiting shelters

–They don’t find the pet that they love

–Their application got rejected because the shelter think it’s not a match

How to solve it?

Assumptions:

–When people visit shelters, the chance they are gonna find a pet is largely increased

–A lot of them actually don’t visit the shelters

–Provide enough information for them would encourage people start visiting

Who can help?

Volunteers for the pets in shelters, folster families, Pet experts, and ChatBot (big data & AI)

What’s the scale of the need?

Facts: Compared to house finding, about 0.5M houses were sold last year in US. For pet finding, 17M people are thinking about having a pet, about 6M pets are added every year, and 2.7M are through adoption

How often do they need it?

Assumptions: Similar to house finding, when people are ready to find a pet companion, they’ll start looking for information and need a conversation or customer service chat with an expert instantly for 30 min to 1 hour. And it could happen daily in 1-2 months. Then untill they are ready for the next pet, they won’t need much of the help for 5-10 years.
